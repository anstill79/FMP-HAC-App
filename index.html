HAC App

Introduction

This is a guide to setting up the HAC App on your computer. The HAC App is a designed to help you work with hearing aids and hearing instruments through all stages of care, from selection to order to delivery to follow-up care.

Setup the HAC App

Download the 'Launch HAC' shortcut from the HAC Teams files section as shown here

Landing Page & Patients List

After logging in you will arrive at the landing page. The Main button takes you to the most commonly-used section.

The Patients list allows you to add new patients or work with existing hearing aid patients. The header has controls for searching and adding new patients. Each row on the screen shows you basic patient info such as name and MRN as well as the most recently-fitted hearing device. There are also controls to add new fitting records to the patient record and a section for managing clinic associations.

Patients List

After adding a new patient or finding the patient via search, you can enter the most recent fitting record by clicking on the patient or device name . If the record is empty you will be asked if you want to create a new fitting record.

Clicking on the MRN will copy it to the clipboard. This is handy for pasting into Epic if needing to open the chart there. Also available is a Clinic Association section which is not commonly used but helps manage filtering behind the scenes so that patients fitted at other clinics can be incorporated into your clinic if the patient is seen at both (or more) locations. Lastly, errors or updates to the demographics can be edited from this screen.

Fitting Record

The fitting record is created at the time of device consultation. The fitting record contains many important dates and details about the recommended devices, clinician, clinic, insurance coverage, as well as follow-up care. Each fitting record contains one or more individual hearing devices.

Wherever possible, the HAC App will fill in data automatically, such as your name and the date or date/time of an action as it is performed. Other details may be auto-entered such as clinic location, license number, etc. Creating a new fitting record should auto-enter many of these details when you create a new fitting record. As other clinic team members work on parts of the hearing aid order, delivery, or follow-up process their names and dates of actions will be logged as well. This is a powerful feature that allows us to quickly see the status of a device order or follow-up in one centralized location. Another powerful feature that relates to centralized data storage is that duplicate data entry is greatly reduced. For example, if one staff member is responsible for checking in new devices and logging the serial numbers, they will enter the SN once and it will populate anywhere we need it, such as on the Purchase Agreement or in a text blob like the one used in Specialty Comments.

Adding Devices

Each device has its own section in the fitting record. You can add new devices by clicking the Add Device button. This will take you to the list of devices available for fitting. You can search the list by manufacturer or model name. When you find the device you want to add, click one of the add buttons to add it to the fitting record for the right ear, left ear, a bilateral fitting, or 'Generic' which can be used for accessories or other non-hearing aid devices.

Device Details

Each device has fields to enter the details for ordering, receipt, fitting, warranties, and other related data. A single device record may be completed by more than one team member, such as device options being entered by the audiologist while order and receipt details are entered by the coordinators. Certain screens will automate some of these data entry tasks, such as applying today's date to the Date Ordered field for multiple devices in a fitting record. Certain fields may also be used later on, after the fitting has taken place, such as return for credit or date of device loss.

Activity

The activity section shows a list of activity related to this fitting. It provides a place to attach comments, notes, updates, or anything else that may be useful in the management of this fitting. The most helpful activity entries involve send-out repairs or other types of repairs that are not scheduled visits with the audiologist. At a glance it is easy to see what was done, when it was sent out or worked on, when it came back, and what needs to happen next.
